How to enable PowerMac G4 MDD 2003 to support SATA disks

Here you'll discover how to flash a cheap SATA controller for mac support.

Buy a cheap SATA controller based on Sil3512

I've got ST-Lab A-390 (Sata150, 2 int/Raid, PCI) for 568 rub. You may also buy a controller with Sil3112 chip too.

Insert your SATA controller card to the PowerMac G4

Download a firmware here

Run a FlashTool

Be careful, this operation may destroy your card. But don't worry, it was cheap! There is option to restore an original BIOS to use it in PC.

Reboot your Mac

It isn't necessary, but if you enter single-mode, you'll see messages containing our new SATA port and it's extension.

Done!

After reboot you won't see any change in the Serial-ATA section:

Don't worry, there are two additional ATA buses that you may use.

You may connect SATA drives to your PowerMac and use it. You may even install the Mac OS X and it will boot from the SATA drive.
